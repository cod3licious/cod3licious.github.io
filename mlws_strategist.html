ML for Data Strategists Workshop

This course is aimed at employees, who have an interest in machine learning, and managers, who want to supervise data science projects.

  • You will get an overview of the topic of machine learning and typical fields of application.
  • You will get to know the data analysis process and understand which problems can be solved with machine learning and where difficulties can arise.

The 2-day workshop follows a flipped classroom format, where the participants study the theory and solve the exercises at their own speed and the results are then discussed in the group sessions.

Prerequisites

  • Good command of the English language (all materials are in English)
  • Basic understanding of math (at high school level)
  • Have fun puzzling and an interest in new technologies
  • Complete the Preliminary Self-Study Part 1 before the first group session

Agenda

The course includes group sessions on two consecutive days and self-study parts before and in between, where you read through the chapters of the book, test yourself with short quizzes, and take notes in the workbook. Your answers to the questions in the workbook are the basis for the group discussions.

Important: Please complete the first self-study part before the first group session!

The group sessions take place remotely via Microsoft Teams, Google Meet, Zoom, Slack, or similar. Please join the calls with your camera turned on so the sessions feel a bit more personal.

Preliminary Self-Study Part 1 (~4h):

Block 1.1:
  • Read the whole chapter: "Introduction"
  • Don't forget to take notes in the workbook (throughout the whole course; → Word template)
  • Answer Quiz 1 (also available as a pdf in case you can't access Google Forms)
Block 1.2:
  • Read the whole chapter: "Data & Preprocessing"
  • Answer Quiz 2 [pdf]
Block 1.3:
  • Read the chapter "ML Solutions: Overview"
  • Answer Quiz 3 [pdf]

Day 1 morning: Group Session 1 (~4h):

  • Introduction
  • Discuss workbook questions from the 1st self-study part
  • Prepare a 90-second Spotlight presentation for one of the given ML use cases. Feel free to prepare a few slides or use the Word template.
  • Spotlight presentations

Day 1 afternoon: Self-Study Part 2 (~3h):

Block 2.1:
  • Read the chapter: "Supervised Learning" (the section on "Deep Learning (Neural Networks)" is optional)
  • Read the whole chapter: "Avoiding Common Pitfalls"
  • Answer Quiz 4 [pdf]
  • Read the whole chapter: "Conclusion"
Block 2.2:
  • Complete the exercise: "Your next ML Project" (in case you need some inspiration for a project idea, have a look at how ML could be used to fight climate change). Feel free to prepare a few slides or use the Word template and aim for a 5 minute presentation.
  • You might want to have a look at the section "Taxonomy of ML problems & solutions" from the Data Scientist version of the book, if you have trouble determining the correct ML approach for your idea

Day 2: Group Session 2 (~6h):

  • Discuss workbook questions from the 2nd self-study part
  • Demonstration: "A Day in the Life of a Data Scientist: Analyzing a Dataset"
  • ML project idea presentations
  • Feedback
